## Why $100 Is Enough

Forget the myth that you need thousands to invest. That's boomer talk. The truth? Apps like Robinhood and Coinbase let you start with literally $1. You don't need a broker. You don't need a degree. You need a phone, an app, and the decision to start.

$100 isn't a lot — but $100 *plus time* is. That's the whole game. The earlier you start, the more time does the heavy lifting for you. Your money earns money, then *that* money earns money. It's called compound growth, and it's the closest thing to free money that exists.

You're not trying to get rich next month. You're building a machine that gets richer over years. Let's build it.

## Step 1: Pick Your App (5 Minutes)

You only need one to start. Here are three that require nothing but a phone and a few taps:

### Robinhood
- **What it is:** Stock and crypto trading app. Dead simple UI.
- **Start with:** Any amount (fractional shares available)
- **Best for:** Buying individual stocks and ETFs yourself
- **Pros:** No fees, fractional shares, clean design, instant deposits
- **Cons:** Easy to gamble if you're not careful. No retirement accounts (IRA) yet on all plans.
- **Download:** App Store or Google Play → sign up → link bank → done in ~10 min

### Coinbase
- **What it is:** The biggest crypto exchange in the US
- **Start with:** $1 minimum
- **Best for:** Getting into Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other crypto
- **Pros:** Super beginner-friendly, educational videos that pay you crypto ("Coinbase Learn")
- **Cons:** Higher fees than some alternatives (use Coinbase Advanced for lower fees). Crypto is volatile — don't put in more than you can afford to lose.
- **Download:** App Store or Google Play → sign up → verify ID → link bank

### Wealthfront
- **What it is:** Robo-advisor. You deposit money, it invests for you automatically.
- **Start with:** $500 (but their high-yield cash account starts at $1)
- **Best for:** Hands-off investing. Set it and forget it.
- **Pros:** Automatic rebalancing, tax-loss harvesting, diversified portfolios
- **Cons:** 0.25% annual fee, $500 minimum for investing (start with their cash account if you're under $500)
- **Download:** App Store or Google Play → sign up → answer a few questions → it invests for you

**Hermes' Recommendation:** Start with **Robinhood** if you want to learn by doing. Start with **Wealthfront** if you want to set it and forget it. Add **Coinbase** later when you want to dabble in crypto.

## Step 2: Pick Your Strategy

You've got three options. Pick the one that matches your vibe:

### A) Index Fund Autopilot 🤖
*Buy one thing. Own a piece of the entire stock market.*

An index fund is a bundle of hundreds (or thousands) of stocks packaged into one purchase. When you buy a share of **VOO** (Vanguard S&P 500 ETF), you own a tiny slice of the 500 biggest companies in America — Apple, Amazon, Google, all of it.

- **How:** Search "VOO" or "SPY" on Robinhood → tap "Buy" → enter amount → done
- **Effort:** Near zero. Check it once a month if you want.
- **Historical return:** ~10% per year on average (not guaranteed, but that's the long-term track record)

### B) Fractional Stock Picking 🎯
*Buy pieces of companies you believe in.*

With fractional shares, you don't need $180 for one share of Apple. You can buy $10 worth. Or $5. Or $1. You pick the companies, you own a piece.

- **How:** Search any stock on Robinhood → tap "Buy" → switch to "Dollars" instead of "Shares" → enter $10 → done
- **Effort:** Medium. You should know what the company does before you buy.
- **Risk:** Higher than index funds. You're betting on individual companies now.

### C) Robo-Advisor Hands-Off 🧘
*Let the robot do everything.*

Wealthfront asks you a few questions (how much risk, what's your goal, when do you need the money) and then builds and manages a portfolio for you. You literally just add money.

- **How:** Sign up → answer the questionnaire → fund your account → it does the rest
- **Effort:** Zero after setup
- **Cost:** 0.25% per year ($0.25 per $100 — it's cheap)

## 5 Mistakes to Avoid 🚫

1. **Trying to time the market.** You can't. Not even professionals do it consistently. Just invest regularly ("dollar-cost averaging") and stop worrying about perfect timing.

2. **Putting everything in crypto.** Crypto can 2x or drop 50% in a week. Fun with $20, catastrophic with $1,000. Keep it to a small slice of your portfolio.

3. **Panic selling when it dips.** The market drops. That's normal. Zoom out — the S&P 500 has recovered from every crash in history. Selling low is how you turn paper losses into real losses.

4. **Checking your portfolio every hour.** Seriously. Set your auto-invest, then go live your life. Check once a week max. Watching the line go up and down will drive you crazy and tempt bad decisions.

5. **Investing money you need.** Only invest money you won't need for at least 1-2 years. Bills, rent, food, and an emergency fund come first. Always.
