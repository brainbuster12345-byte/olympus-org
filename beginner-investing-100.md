### 1. High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA)

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Expected Return** | 4.00% – 5.00% APY |
| **Minimum to Start** | $0 – $1 |
| **Best Apps** | **SoFi** (4.00% APY), **Marcus by Goldman Sachs** (4.00%+ APY), **Ally Bank** (4.00% APY), **Wealthfront Cash** (4.00% APY) |
| **Beginner-Friendly** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5) |

**How it works:** You deposit money. It earns interest. That's it. FDIC insured up to $250,000.

**On $100:** You'd earn ~$4.00–$5.00 per year. Not exciting, but **zero risk of losing your money**.

✅ **Pros:**
- FDIC insured — your money is safe
- Instant to open (5 min on phone)
- Withdraw anytime — no lock-up
- Perfect emergency fund parking spot

❌ **Cons:**
- Returns barely beat inflation
- Interest rates can drop when Fed cuts rates
- Not really "investing" — more like smart saving

**Verdict:** *Everyone should have one of these. Start here before anything else.*

### 2. Treasury Bills / Treasury Notes (via App)

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Expected Return** | 4.25% – 4.75% (T-Bills, short term) |
| **Minimum to Start** | $10 (some platforms) |
| **Best Apps** | **Public.com** (Treasury account), **Public** auto-invests in T-Bills, **Fidelity** (buy T-Bills directly in app), **Schwab** |
| **Beginner-Friendly** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5) |

**How it works:** You're lending money to the U.S. government. They pay you back with interest. The safest investment on Earth.

**On $100:** You'd earn ~$4.25–$4.75 per year with near-zero risk.

✅ **Pros:**
- Backed by the U.S. government — virtually risk-free
- Interest is state-tax-free
- Short-term options (4-week, 8-week T-Bills) so money isn't locked long

❌ **Cons:**
- Slightly more complex than a savings account
- Need to hold until maturity for guaranteed return
- Returns will drop if/when Fed cuts rates

**Verdict:** *A step up from HYSA. Great if you want government-backed returns and don't mind a tiny bit more complexity.*

### 3. Certificates of Deposit (CDs) — App-Based

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Expected Return** | 4.00% – 4.75% APY |
| **Minimum to Start** | $0 – $500 |
| **Best Apps** | **Ally Bank** (no minimum), **Marcus by Goldman Sachs** ($500 min for best rates), **Discover** ($2,500 min), **Sofi** |
| **Beginner-Friendly** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5) |

**How it works:** You lock your money for a set period (3 months to 5 years). You get a guaranteed rate. FDIC insured.

**On $100:** Ally's no-minimum CDs let you start with $100. ~$4.00–$4.75 per year on a 12-month CD.

✅ **Pros:**
- Guaranteed return — you know exactly what you'll earn
- FDIC insured
- Forces you not to touch the money (good for discipline)

❌ **Cons:**
- Early withdrawal penalties (usually 60–90 days of interest)
- Money is locked up — less flexible than HYSA
- Some platforms need $500+ for the best rates

**Verdict:** *Good if you know you won't need the money for a while. Otherwise, just use a HYSA.*

### 4. Fractional Shares of Index Funds / ETFs

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Expected Return** | 7% – 10% per year (historical average, not guaranteed) |
| **Minimum to Start** | $1 |
| **Best Apps** | **Fidelity** (fractional shares, no fees), **Schwab** (Stock Slices), **Robinhood**, **SoFi Invest** |
| **Beginner-Friendly** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5) |

**Top picks for $100:**

| Fund | Ticker | Expense Ratio | What It Is |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vanguard S&P 500 ETF | **VOO** | 0.03% | Top 500 U.S. companies |
| Schwab U.S. Broad Market ETF | **SCHB** | 0.03% | Entire U.S. stock market |
| Vanguard Total World Stock ETF | **VT** | 0.07% | Every stock market on Earth |
| iShares Core S&P 500 ETF | **IVV** | 0.03% | Same as VOO, different brand |

**How it works:** You buy a tiny slice of an ETF that holds hundreds or thousands of stocks. One tap, instant diversification.

**On $100:** At 8% average annual return → ~$8/year. But with compounding, that $100 becomes $216 in 10 years (without adding more).

✅ **Pros:**
- Instant diversification — you own a piece of the whole market
- Historically 7-10% average annual returns over long periods
- Fractional shares mean $100 works just as well as $10,000
- No need to pick individual stocks
- Low fees (0.03% = $0.03 per $100/year)

❌ **Cons:**
- Can lose money in the short term (market drops happen)
- Returns are NOT guaranteed
- Need to hold for years (ideally 5+) to ride out volatility
- Tempting to panic sell when it drops

**Verdict:** *The single best thing a beginner can do with $100. Boring? Yes. Effective? Extremely.*

### 5. Robo-Advisors

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Expected Return** | 5% – 9% per year (after fees, varies by risk level) |
| **Minimum to Start** | $0 – $500 |
| **Best Apps** | **Wealthfront** ($500 min), **Betterment** ($0 min), **Fidelity Go** ($0 min for balances under $25K), **Schwab Intelligent Portfolios** ($5,000 min) |
| **Beginner-Friendly** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5) |

**How it works:** You answer a few questions. The robot builds and manages a diversified portfolio for you. It rebalances automatically. Set it and forget it.

**On $100:** Best option is **Betterment** or **Fidelity Go** (both $0 minimum). Betterment charges 0.25%/year = $0.25 on $100.

✅ **Pros:**
- Completely hands-off — they do everything
- Automatic rebalancing and tax-loss harvesting (Wealthfront/Betterment)
- Built for people who don't want to learn investing
- Low minimums

❌ **Cons:**
- Management fees eat into returns (0.25%–0.35%/year)
- You can do the same thing yourself with VOO + BND for free
- Limited customization
- Schwab's best option needs $5,000

**Verdict:** *Perfect if you want zero decision-making. You pay a tiny fee for someone (something) else to handle it all.*

### 6. Target-Date Funds

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Expected Return** | 6% – 9% per year (historical) |
| **Minimum to Start** | $1 – $0 (varies) |
| **Best Apps** | **Fidelity** (Fidelity Freedom Index funds), **Schwab**, **Vanguard** |
| **Beginner-Friendly** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5) |

**How it works:** Pick a fund based on when you'll need the money (e.g., "Target 2065" if you're retiring around then). It automatically shifts from aggressive to conservative as you age.

✅ **Pros:**
- One fund, fully diversified, auto-adjusts over time
- True "set and forget"
- Available in any brokerage

❌ **Cons:**
- Slightly higher expense ratios than pure index funds (0.10%–0.15%)
- Less control over allocation
- May be too conservative for young investors

**Verdict:** *Great for retirement accounts (Roth IRA). Simplest possible long-term investment.*

### 7. Individual Stocks (Fractional Shares)

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Expected Return** | -100% to +50%+ (wildly variable) |
| **Minimum to Start** | $1 |
| **Best Apps** | **Robinhood** (most popular), **Fidelity**, **Schwab**, **SoFi**, **Public.com** |
| **Beginner-Friendly** | ⭐⭐ (2/5) |

**On $100:** You could buy fractional shares of Apple, Google, Amazon, etc. One share of Apple (~$230) = you own 43% of one share with $100.

✅ **Pros:**
- Can pick companies you believe in
- Fractional shares make expensive stocks accessible
- Educational — you learn how markets work
- Commission-free on all major apps

❌ **Cons:**
- Individual stocks are **much riskier** than index funds
- Most beginner stock pickers underperform the market
- Emotional decision-making (FOMO, panic selling)
- Research takes real time and skill

**Verdict:** *If you want to try this, keep it to 10-20% of your $100. Put the rest in index funds.*

### 9. Options Trading (For Awareness Only)

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Expected Return** | -100% of investment to unlimited upside |
| **Minimum to Start** | $1+ (but realistically $500+ to do anything meaningful) |
| **Best Apps** | **Robinhood**, **Webull**, **Fidelity** |
| **Beginner-Friendly** | ⭐ (1/5) |

⚠️ **Athena's Warning:** This is listed for awareness, NOT recommendation. ~90% of beginner options traders lose money. Options expire worthless if the trade goes against you. With $100, you can only afford very short-dated, out-of-the-money contracts — which is essentially gambling.

✅ **Pros:**
- Leverage — control more shares with less money
- Can hedge existing positions
- Educational to understand

❌ **Cons:**
- Most beginners lose their entire investment
- Complex — Greeks, expiration, strike prices, etc.
- Time decay works against you
- Not a place for $100 you care about

**Verdict:** *Learn about options. Do not trade them with your first $100. Come back when you have $5,000+ and paper-traded for 6 months.*

## 🚫 THINGS TO AVOID WITH $100

- **Penny stocks** — Manipulated, illiquid, most go to zero
- **Meme coins** (Dogecoin, Shiba Inu, etc.) — Gambling, not investing
- **Forex trading** — 70-80% of retail traders lose money
- **Binary options** — Literally banned in many countries
- **"Guru" courses** costing $500+ — Free info exists everywhere
- **Any platform asking for your SSN via text** — Scam
- **NFTs** — Market has cratered, liquidity near zero for most
